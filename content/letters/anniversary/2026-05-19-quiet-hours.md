---
title: Quiet Hours
note: Written the morning of.
---

Dear Nicole,

It's six in the morning and the kettle is doing its small operatic thing on the stove. I have been awake for an hour because I wanted to be the one to hear the day first today, which is a strange ambition but feels like the right one.

You are still asleep in the next room, and from here I can see the bend of your shoulder and the part of the duvet you always claim is yours. You are wrong about that, of course. The duvet is communal. I will keep losing this argument graciously.

Three years. I keep wanting to find some new way to say it but the only honest version is the boring one — *it has gone fast and I am grateful*. That's it. That's the whole sentence.

I made coffee. It is too strong. You will complain. I will pretend to be sorry.

Happy anniversary.

m.
