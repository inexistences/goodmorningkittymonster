title: 8th Year
note: you will always be cats

I was a boy, at first.
A boy who grew up under conservative christian parents. A boy protected from the world, but with an insatiable appetite to interact with it.

You were a girl, at first.
A girl whose rebellion won her a lip piercing at a young age. A girl who partied and broke into haunted hospitals and lived life to the fullest.

I was in love, at first.
I was in love with your hair, with your eyes, with your clothes. Then with your charm, with your spirit, with your words. Then with your self, with your all, with your soul.

That process took a few months. This relationship took a few years. I’m no longer a boy to you, and you’re no longer a girl to me. At some point, I, self, us, you, we, it all became confusing.

I lost the thread of where I ended and where you began. A weave of neurons, clusters of memories attached to each other by synapses, firing thousands of memories into each other each second. Every location holds your name- you are this room. You are Tampines, Bedok, Tanah Merah, and Starbucks. You are Berlin, Gengenbach, Budapest, and Prague. You are the Californian coast and the Redwood forest. You are foxes. You are dogs. You are cows. You are cats.

Then.
That is when I understood.
You were everything to me.

Happy 8th year together, love.
