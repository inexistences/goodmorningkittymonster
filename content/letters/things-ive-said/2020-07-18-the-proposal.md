---
title: The proposal
note: this will always be one of the happiest moments of my life
---

I’ve always been a bit of a romantic, so while I never spent much time thinking about the future, I did occasionally look forward to proposing and getting married. And for much of my youth, it was a simple but consistent vision: the proposal would be some combination of kneeling to ask the question / a ring / celebrating with friends / some elaborately planned surprise. Fast forward to the present, and I did it lying down, without a ring, with no friends present. The only thing I got ‘right’ was that it was a surprise - but it was a bit of a surprise to me as well as to you.

If it all works out the way you want it to, proposals are something you only get to do this once in your life. Yet what I eventually did was nothing at all like what I had, for years, wanted it and thought it would be. So, by most accounts, in terms of meeting expectations, it could be described as a failure.

But it was the most beautiful and meaningful failure I ever could have asked for.

The only thing I failed at was to live up to my younger self’s desires. But that younger self isn’t me. The only expectations I betrayed were those of a different life - of the person I was before I became the person I am. That person could have gone to law school and might already have children. That person bought jewelry for his future partner in some ridiculously romantic (sorry, ridiculous and romantic) gesture. I don’t want to be that person. I want to be this person, the one who discovered himself with you by my side, this intellectual but silly, charming but corny, imperfect person who’s made perfectly happy with you.

And for this person, an unexpected proposal might not have been so unexpected.

I want my life with you to be unpredictable in the best possible ways. So that quiet moment, that surprise-less surprise, it’s exactly who I want to be with you. It’s exactly the best of who I am, and it’s exactly representative of the two of us: without fanfare or grandness, but true, caring, and honest.

I’m rationally and logically driven. Proposing to you on acid has been one of the most irrational and illogical things I’ve ever done. So congrats, I guess. You beat the system. You exert such an influence on me that you can make me act against my very nature.

If that isn’t love, then what it is?
