Hey kitty,

I guess 2025 has been a pretty stressful year for you, seen through the lens of our relationship.

But I feel like I’ve talked enough about that for now, and we can have as many follow-up conversations as we need in person later. So instead, I just wanted to take the time to talk to you in the medium that is now most comfortable to me: semi-long-form letter :p

Life is weird, huh? And difficult. It rarely gives you what you want, and rarely when you want it or feel ready to receive it. Sometimes these things are bad, and sometimes they’re great. Oftentimes, they’re objectively neither– but just start to feel one way because of the prior expectations we hold about how things should be. Like... thinking something should go a certain way doesn't make it more likely to happen that way. It just creates this gap between expectation and reality that I have to grieve every time things don't line up. And that grief is optional, you know? For a long time I’ve still wanted you to be a slightly different version of yourself, someone that engages more with me in ways that I want rather than you are. But honestly, I’ve known you for long enough to recognise that you’re fundamentally someone who doesn’t do well with significant change, you’re fiercely independent and stubborn in all the best (and worst) ways. I like and love who you are, but I think I’ve been focusing too much on who you could be. Constantly looking at that gap of course starts to feel draining. All I see is the empty space between the person you are and the imaginary outline that I wished you could be. The relationship we actually have is different from what I imagined, but that doesn't make it wrong… just real. And I’m sure I’m different from the idea you have of who I could be also: someone more organised, more proactive, more sexual. And those are things you might also have to grieve. But they’re also what makes me real.

That’s not to say I don’t want to become more the person you want me to be, nor put in the effort. That’s the incredible thing about love: it pushes us to be better people for someone else, in ways that make us better people even for ourselves. And I hope you’re also open to changing in ways that move towards my ideal too. But without expectation, without punishment for future failures, that if either one of us come short it doesn’t matter. It doesn’t matter as long as we’re still trying.

I’m really glad you found Nathan. I can tell that he adds a lot to your life in ways that I don’t currently (and most likely ever can). And I’m glad I met Melina. She does so similarly for me. I know it’s not the same situation on both sides, but that was never the point. I’m not trying to give you more of what I want for myself to lessen the guilt. I just want you to live as full a life as you can, while I similarly try to live mine. I know my life philosophy isn’t compatible with yours in some pretty destabilising ways, and I’m sorry about that. But that’s just who I am now, and I don’t want to continue living having to hide that from you. I deserve better than that, so do you, and so does the relationship that we’ve worked so hard to build together over the last nearly-16 years. And just as I'm asking you to see me as I am now, I want to understand who you're becoming. What this has meant for you, what's changed in your inner world, what you're discovering about the life you want.

I hope that in retrospect you’ve valued (or will eventually value) this time apart that we’ve had. I hope that when I come back I’ll be able to meet you again, not as who I’ve been but as who I am now. And I trust that you’ll meet me with the same unapologetic honesty – which should be easier for you, because that’s closer to who you’ve always been than I.

So, yes, 2025 was complicated, but I also think, necessary. And no matter what the future brings, you will always have been a huge part of my life, and a huge one moving forward. I’ll fight for that, no matter what shape it takes. You’re still my partner and best friend, and I don’t take those things lightly. I hope 2026 lets us have many difficult conversations that bring us closer to what we originally were: just two teenagers who saw something incredible and attractive in the other, and decided to walk that path and see wherever it leads. But also towards something we’ve never been: two adults who have earned each other’s trust through repeated failures and clear communication, even when we're becoming people our younger selves wouldn't recognize. Even as two people who continue choosing each other while also choosing ourselves.

With deep love,

Your kitty.
