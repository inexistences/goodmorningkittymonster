---
title: Commemorating GP
note: Recorded after the fact — close to verbatim.
---

At the foot of the staircase, you pull open the black gate.

You start to ascend the stairs, one by one, as you always have. Six years of climbing and descending, a daily recursive ritual, never punctuated by the least bit of difference.

You are Grey Project’s gallery manager. Six years of familiarity with these dusty grey walls and this dimly-lit staircase. A wooden postbox that somehow still receives mail meant for previous tenants, now almost a decade gone. You are oddly familiar with this interim space, neither street nor gallery. A tunnel of quiet between origin and destination, the world outside and work. Today, your feet tread a little heavier. The gravity between steps increases almost imperceptibly, just enough for you to notice that by the last step, this trek took longer than usual. Maybe it was you, your subconscious mind slowing you down just long enough to capture this last ascent in richer detail. Maybe it was the space, extending invisible tendrils around you, as if trying to extend the time you spend in it. But you ascend nonetheless. You reach the apex of the stairs and wonder to yourself if this hyper-sensitivity to your surroundings will last all day. As if you had to treasure the next five hours beyond all others, marking the ending of something of great importance.

You haven’t even stepped into the gallery yet.
 So you enter. Close the door behind you.
 The corridor waits.
 Hours pass, although they seem to alternate between taking minutes and years. The door opens, then closes behind you as you step out.
 The corridor waits.

You are no longer Grey Project’s gallery manager. Six years of familiarity with these dusty grey walls and this dimly lit staircase, but they are no longer yours. You entered a host but you leave a guest. You place your foot on the top step. You’ve never done this before. You’ve never left Grey Projects as an observer. Is this what visitors feel when they leave after a show? You take another step. The air is light but heavy. You are happy but sad. Most of all you are a little lost. You know you need to take the next step, both figuratively and literally, but both of these tasks seem almost alien to you.

What does it feel like to break-up with a worthy cause?
 What does it feel like to say goodbye to a relationship that has run its course?
 You realise that it feels like life. It feels like a path walked. It feels like goodbye, but more like the French would say it. Au Revoir. Until I see you again. You have overseen a multitude of efforts over the years, but you’re only now realising that you too have been a work-in-progress, refined by your responsibilities and efforts and sleepless nights. You too have been forged and tempered. You are a product of your history at Grey Projects, and no matter what happens next, always will be.

You continue to descend the stairs, one by one, as you always have. Six years of climbing and descending, a daily recursive ritual,
finally punctuated with a little, but important, difference.
