---
title: The Ascension of colour
note: Oreos, 29 September 2019 at Dakota, written on 30 Sep / 1 Oct
---

We calmly awaited the ascension of colour.

The journey began slowly, surrounded by awkwardness, shrubbery, and stars. We watched the parade of idiocy and awkwardness pass by, and were moved to grin, to smile, and to laugh. But the world too, was moved.

It undulated before us, shifting, breathing, evaporating colours, dividing white into red, blue, and green, and below it all, an infinite web of violet. The clouds became splashes of slowly poured milk in the sky, and the city became an island of distant neon lights.

The ascension was complete. We were ready for beauty.

We chose to lose ourselves in art. We travelled through mirrored temples. Through circuses,  bars, ballrooms, burning streets, and high schools. Visions lifted us up. War broke me down.
We marveled at the duality of desire and repulsion. We marveled at everything.
Exhausted of artistic vision, we chose to marvel at each other instead.
Our minds devoured each other, although our bodies were slightly less up to the task at hand. So we changed task. We repurposed our motion from horizontal to vertical, and joined the world around us in movement. Eyes closed, naked, shameless, we expressed ourselves freely, carried by incessant dark rhythms that first enveloped us on the ocean floor.
We danced this way for hours until the world and sound around us stopped moving. It left us together, so very close together, until, at last, silence swallowed everything but the sound of our breaths at each others ears, our arms, bodies, and hearts and thoughts intertwined just a little tighter than was possible the night before.
